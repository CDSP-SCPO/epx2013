PRICES FOR AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS AND RELATED MEASURES FOR 1998/1999

The Council had a first exchange of views on the Commission proposals concerning the 1998/1999 Agricultural Price Package.

The debate was introduced by Commissioner Fischler who stressed that, in view of the forthcoming reforms under the Agenda 2000 communication of July 1997, the proposals largely amount to a rollover of existing arrangements, with a few exceptions required for strictly legal reasons or to fulfil its prior commitments. He pointed out that the proposed price package is in line with the Commission's orientation towards a freeze of the agricultural budget for 1999 in comparison with 1998.

Ministers gave their initial reaction to the proposals and raised a number of questions (notably the reduction in the level of aid for hemp and the freeze of the ceilings of special premiums in the beef sector) or mentioned aspects which, according to them, needed to be solved urgently (such as the rate of set aside for arable crops). A number of Ministers also mentioned specific sectors, notably milk, wine and olive oil, for which they felt reform was urgent.

In conclusion, the Council noted the preliminary observations made by Member States and instructed the Special Committee on Agriculture to examine the proposals in detail and report back as quickly as possible on the results in order to allow for a decision to be taken once the Opinion of the European Parliament is available.

 

 

 

RAW TOBACCO : COMMON MARKET ORGANIZATION (CMO)

The Council heard a presentation by Commissioner Fischler of the Commission's proposal to reform the common organization of the market in raw tobacco and held a first debate on the subject.

Commissioner Fischler stressed that the proposal aims at directing tobacco production in the Union towards quality and market requirements while safeguarding, at the same time, its important socio-economic role in the producing regions.

During the debate, the vast majority of delegations agreed on the need for reform of the common market organization, although their positions varied considerably on the approach to be taken and on the extent of such a reform.

In conclusion, the Council noted the preliminary responses of Member States and asked the Special Committee on Agriculture to examine the proposal further and report back as soon as possible on the results in order to allow for a decision to be taken once the Opinion of the European Parliament has been delivered.

 

 

 

BEEF AND VEAL : MEASURES TO PROMOTE QUALITY AND PUBLICIZE LABELLING

The Council examined a Presidency compromise concerning the Commission's amended proposal for measures to promote quality and publicize the labelling of beef and veal.

In view of the positions of delegations, the Council noted that agreement was not possible at this stage. Under these circumstances, the Presidency will reflect further on the follow up to be given to this dossier.

It is recalled that the proposal envisages a strengthening of the existing scheme to promote quality Community beef and veal as well as new measures to publicise to consumers the new labelling arrangements adopted by the Council in April 1997 and new measures to promote quality Community beef and veal in third countries.

Following the European Parliament's Opinion, the Commission presented a modified proposal amending the original legal base (Article 43 of the Treaty) by adding Article 129a on consumer protection. As a consequence, in order to adopt the Regulation on the basis of Article 43 alone, the Council will have to act unanimously.

 

 

 

 

 

OECD MINISTERIAL MEETING OF THE COMMITTEE FOR AGRICULTURE

The Council:

- assessed, on the basis of information given by Mr Fischler and by the Netherlands delegation, the progress achieved to date in the preparation of the OECD Ministerial Meeting of the Committee for Agriculture to be held in Paris on 5/6 March 1998;

- underlined the importance of the above Meeting at a critical time in the run-up to important external negotiations, and the necessity to maintain a united front in support of the Commission;

- to this end, noted the reference made by most delegations to the Council conclusions of November 1997 on "Agenda 2000" which they consider a useful starting point;

- agreed to ensure appropriate and close Community coordination in preparation for the Ministerial meeting's final Communiqué;

- noted that, to this effect, further Community coordination will be held in the presence of high-level officials in Brussels, on 23 February 1998.

It is recalled that the forthcoming meeting of OECD Committee for Agriculture will conduct

- a reflection on the achievements to date on commitments to reform agricultural policies taken by OECD Ministers in 1987 and 1992;

- an assessment of the impact of the major domestic and international policy reforms undertaken in the last few years including the Uruguay Round Agreement on Agriculture;

- an exchange of views on the important decisions to be taken on policy priorities for OECD agriculture in the period ahead.

 

 

FUNCTIONING OF THE EWE PREMIUM : SECOND COMMISSION REPORT

The Council took note of the Commission's report and of the positions adopted by a number of Member States on its conclusions. These stated in particular that, having evaluated the functioning of the ewe premium, the Commission did not consider it necessary to amend the basic Council regulation at this stage.

Furthermore, the Council noted that the Commission has endeavoured to find appropriate solutions, within its independent powers, to most of the concerns expressed by Member States with the exception of the requested introduction of a top-up premium for ewes as well as of regional quotas, to which the Commission remains firmly opposed.

 

 

BANANAS : COMMON MARKET ORGANIZATION (CMO)

The Council :

- took note of an interim report of the Special Committee on Agriculture concerning the Commission proposal amending Regulation (EEC) No 404/93 on the common organization of the market in bananas;

- had an exchange of views during which most delegations clarified and completed their positions on the subject;

- recognized that the reformed CMO for bananas must be WTO compatible; took note of the Commission's statement that its Legal Service considers it being the case; noted that, following the request of a number of delegations, the Legal Services of the Council will come forward with advice as soon as possible;

- noted that Member States have confirmed the aim of reaching a final decision before the end of June 1998, provided that the European Parliament's Opinion is available, the Presidency having noted the widespread readiness to work on the basis of the Commission proposal;

- instructed accordingly the Special Committee on Agriculture to continue speedily the detailed examination of the proposal, notably on such issues as:

= the autonomous quota and proposed tariff rate,

= licensing,

= the situation of EU and ACP producers,

= Fair Trade Bananas.

- asked for a report to be presented to the Council as soon as possible.

It is recalled that the proposal aims to bring the Community banana regime into line with the European Union's commitments vis-à-vis the World Trade Organization (WTO) before the 1 January 1999 deadline. In its new proposal, the Commission aims to maintain the tariff quota at its current level of 2,2 million tonnes, and at the same rate of duty – currently ECU 75/tonne; to set an additional autonomous tariff quota of 353 000 tonnes at a rate of ECU 300/tonne in order to take account of the enlargement of the EU; to abolish the current provisions on import certificates and replace them by a system compatible with the WTO; to grant a share of the tariff quota to suppliers having a substantial interest and, consequently, not to allocate to individual countries shares within the total allocation to traditional African, Caribbean and Pacific (ACP) suppliers. The Commission also intends, within its own sphere of competence, to ensure that the income of Community producers is maintained.

 

 

AGREEMENT ON VETERINARY EQUIVALENCE WITH THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

The Council:

- was informed by Mr. Fischler of the outcome of the high-level discussions held in Washington with regard to the major problems to be resolved before negotiations on the veterinary agreement can be concluded, namely: recognition of the Union's animal health status and its regionalization policy, the United States' new Mega-Reg rules and the ban on imports of live bovines and bovine products because of BSE;

- took note of the progress made and of the remarks from some delegations;

- stressed the need to receive rapidly from the American authorities the letters which should set out the possible solutions identified in Washington;

- agreed to return to this issue at its next meeting for a substantive debate on the basis of these letters with a view to reaching, if possible, a final decision;

- therefore instructed Coreper:

= to evaluate carefully, as soon as they are available, the contents of the letters which are expected from the USA;

= to finalize the draft veterinary agreement.

AGRICULTURE

International Agreements

The Council adopted :

- a Decision authorizing the Commission to negotiate a successor agreement to the 1995 International Grains Agreement on behalf of the Community;

- a Decision authorizing the Commission to conduct negotiations with a view to the conclusion of a Protocol on veterinary matters to supplement the Agreement between the European Community, of the one part, and the Government of Denmark and the Home Government of the Faroe Islands, of the other part.

 

Seeds

The Council adopted an amendment to Decision n° 95/514/EC on the equivalence of field inspections carried out in third countries on seed-producing crops and on the equivalence of seed produced in third countries prolonging the validity of the Decision from 31 December 1997 to 31 December 1999.

 

Animal welfare during transport *

Following the political agreement reached in December 1997 (see Press Release n°  13138/97-Presse 387-G), the Council adopted a Regulation setting out additional animal protection standards applicable to road vehicles used for the carriage of livestock on journeys exceeding eight hours. The implementation of this Regulation in the Member States should take place as from 1st July 1999.

 

Cereals trade

The Council adopted conclusions and recommendations on the Special Report n° 5/97 of the Court of Auditors on management of the Community cereals trade involving export refunds, special import arrangements and regional aid schemes. In particular, the Council :

– asked the Member States to examine critically the operation of their own scrutiny systems;

– invited the Commission:

= to rationalize the administration of the cereals refund system;

= to play a more active role in the co-ordination of measures to monitor multinational firms, especially in the case of subsidiaries based in non-member countries;

= to look into the possibility of applying an "anti-fraud" clause in trade agreements to be concluded with non-member countries;

= to keep the Council regularly informed of the outcome of actions to recover amounts wrongly paid out;

= as soon as possible to revise the calculation of the aid for the supply of cereals to the Azores under the POSEIMA scheme.

 

CUSTOMS UNION

Agreement between the EEC and Cyprus

The Council authorized the Commission to negotiate, on behalf of the Community, with Cyprus with a view to completing the existing agreement between the EEC and Cyprus, which dates back to 1972, by an additional protocol regarding mutual administrative assistance in customs matters.

 

INTERNAL MARKET

Statistics *

The Council unanimously approved an amendment to Regulation 58/97 concerning structural business statistics, extending the scope of the Directive to the insurance sector.

Regulation 58/97 establishes a common framework for the collection, compilation, transmission and evaluation of Community statistics on the structure, activity, competitiveness and performance of business in the Community. A new annex extends to the insurance sector the obligation to supply comparable and harmonized statistics.
